Kegeyli

Read more

The origin of Kegeyli is associated with the Kegeyli canal, which flows through the town itself and through the district of Kegeyli also. Both sides of this canal are called Kegeyli because they were surrounded by kegay trees and turquoise. This was later generalized to the town.
Kegeyli district was created in 2004 by the merger of former Boʻzatov district and former Kegeyli district.
Most of the population is Karakalpak, but there are also Uzbeks, Kazakhs, Tatars, Koreans, Russians and others. In average there are 62 people per km2.

Muratbay Nizanov

Read more

Muratbay Nizanov was born in 1951 in the Kegeyli district. After graduating from high school, he studied at the Karakalpak State University. He worked as a laborer on his first farm. He began his career writing poetry. He has written several movie scripts such as "Krort", "Urı", "Tank". You can watch the movie via this link.

Short story about "Shibliy ata"
A man goes to Tajimahal. Someone comes up to you and asks where you came from. He replied that he was from Kegeyli district of Karakalpakstan. He then asks him if he has gone to the Shibli ata shrine. He replies, "No." Then why didn't you go here first, because it is said that visiting Shibli ata is the same as visiting Tajimahal.

Flying saucer

On the evening of January 1, 1989, a flying saucer flew to the village of Kuybishev, Kegeyli district. The man who saw him said that he was about 2 meters 10 centimeters tall and had odd number of eyes. The incident appeared in all the newspapers at that time.
